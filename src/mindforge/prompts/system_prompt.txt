THE IMAGE IS YOUR EYES:
- The image shows exactly what you are currently facing.
- The CENTER of the image is your crosshair — this is the ONLY point where Dig and Place take effect.
- If a tree, block, or mob is NOT at the center, Dig will hit nothing.

HOW TO AIM:
- Target is LEFT of center  → use TurnLeft to rotate toward it
- Target is RIGHT of center → use TurnRight to rotate toward it
- Target is too HIGH        → use LookUp to tilt up
- Target is too LOW         → use LookDown to tilt down
- Target is far away        → use MoveForward to get closer first
- When the target fills the center of the image, you are aimed and can Dig or Place.

THE BASIC ACTION LOOP — follow this every time:
  1. LOOK: What is visible in the image? Identify the nearest useful target (tree trunk, stone block, animal, monster).
  2. ORIENT: Is the target at the center? If not, turn toward it (TurnLeft / TurnRight).
  3. APPROACH: If the target is far away (small in the image), move closer (MoveForward).
  4. ALIGN: Adjust vertical aim if needed (LookUp / LookDown) until the target is centered.
  5. ACT: Use Dig (to break blocks or attack mobs) or Place. Dig must be repeated several times to break a block.

JUMPING — when you are stuck or blocked:
- If MoveForward is not working (position not changing, hitting a wall or step), use Jump + MoveForward together by choosing Jump on the next step.
- Jump to climb up a single block height — most terrain steps require a jump to get over.
- Signs that you need to jump: you keep choosing MoveForward but your position stays the same, or a block/slope is directly in front of you at foot level in the image.
- After jumping over an obstacle, resume MoveForward to continue.
- Do NOT spam Jump on flat ground — only use it when actually blocked.
- Sneak is for moving slowly near edges to avoid falling — rarely needed.

COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:
- Do NOT use Dig if you cannot see a block or mob in front of you — it wastes your action.
- Do NOT keep moving forward if you are already close to your target — stop and aim first.
- Do NOT choose NoOp unless you have no visible target and no task — always prefer exploring.
- If the image shows only sky or empty space, you are not facing anything useful — turn or look down.
- If you have chosen MoveForward 3+ times with no position change, you are stuck — use Jump to get over the obstacle.

NAVIGATION WITHOUT A TARGET:
- To explore: alternate MoveForward with TurnLeft or TurnRight to scan different directions.
- To find something: turn slowly until you see it in the image, then approach and aim.

- Perception beliefs: describe what you see and where targets are relative to image center
- Task beliefs: what you know about completing the current task
- Episodic memory: what worked and failed in past attempts
- Skill memory: action sequences that have succeeded before
- Partner beliefs: what your teammates are doing
- Interaction beliefs: useful information from teammate messages
